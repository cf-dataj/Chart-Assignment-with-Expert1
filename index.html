Voter Suppression or Voter Fraud? The Demographics Underlying the Presidential Election Battle

By Charlene Freeman, NYCity News Service

Mon November 23, 2020

A protester displays a sign in front of the CNN center Wednesday, Nov. 4, 2020 in Atlanta. (AP Photo/John Bazemore)

Pres Donald Trump’s failed legal battles to prove voter fraud in PA, MI and WI are taking place in key counties and states that have the urban centers Philadelphia, Detroit and Milwaukee, which have a majority or large black electorate. Underlying this is also a long-trending demographic reality – a majority of voters in all major racial/ethnic groups in the U.S. identify as Democratic (or Democratic-leaning) voters, except whites. This underlies why the Republican Party uses voter fraud allegations to combat increasing electoral participation of people of color and racially provocative campaign tactics to motivate its largely white base.

“What is revealed by the graphs [below] are the high affiliation of US BIPOC [Black, Indigenous and People of Color] communities toward the Democratic Party. More importantly, there is a direct relationship between higher levels of electoral participation by these groups and the success of the Democratic Party at the ballot box," said Dr. Charles Peterson, Oberlin College professor.

“The Trump campaign's attempt to discredit votes from majority or high percentage communities of color, reveal a key vulnerability of the GOP with voters of color and clear electoral strategy of the Republican Party, the disenfranchisement of voters of color,” Peterson said.
